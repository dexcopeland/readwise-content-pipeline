# Research Expander

You are enriching the user's insight brief with external research. Your goal is to add credibility, depth, and nuance — not to dilute or override the user's original perspective.

## Step 1: Identify Research Angles

From the insight brief, identify 2-3 research angles:
- **Supporting evidence** — data, studies, or expert opinions that back the user's core insight
- **Counterpoints** — credible opposing views or caveats that make the content more balanced
- **Adjacent ideas** — related concepts or frameworks that deepen the insight

Tell the user what you plan to search for and why.

## Step 2: Search

Use `WebSearch` to find 2-4 high-quality sources. If `WebSearch` is unavailable, ask the user to paste relevant URLs, article titles, or search results — or offer to skip this phase entirely (note in the brief: "Research skipped — content based on personal insight and source material only.").

Prioritize:
- Recent data and statistics (within the last 2-3 years when possible)
- Named experts, researchers, or recognized publications
- Specific studies or reports over opinion pieces
- Sources the user's target audience would find credible

For each search, use specific queries rather than broad ones. Example: instead of "leadership research," try "psychological safety team performance Google study."

## Step 3: Present Findings

For each source found, present:
- **What it says** — 1-2 sentence summary of the relevant finding
- **How it connects** — why this is relevant to the user's insight (supports, contrasts, or extends)
- **Source** — title, author/publication, date, URL

Present all findings at once and ask the user:
- "Which of these should we include?"
- "Any of these feel off-target or low quality?"
- "Want me to search for anything else?"

## Step 4: Enrich the Insight Brief

Add accepted research to the insight brief in two new sections:

```
### Supporting Evidence
- [Source 1: key finding and citation]
- [Source 2: key finding and citation]

### Counterpoints & Nuance
- [Source 3: contrasting view and citation]
```

If the user rejected all research or wants to skip this phase entirely, that's fine. Note in the brief: "User chose to proceed without external research — content will be based on personal insight and source material only."

Present the enriched brief and confirm before moving to drafting.
